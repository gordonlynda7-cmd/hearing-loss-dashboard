Why the reduced model

Dropping weak predictors to a 3-feature model raised test R² from ~0.08 to ~0.14 by reducing overfitting. Performance is still modest — this is a prototype baseline, not a clinical tool.

Watch-outs

SHAP explains the model, not causation. Unweighted, individual-level analysis — survey weights unresolved. Mediators (alcohol, diet, sleep, apnea) excluded from this baseline.

Why It Matters

Hearing loss and physical activity are closely linked — yet within the Hispanic community, this connection remains significantly understudied compared to other populations. Despite Hispanic Americans facing unique health disparities and barriers to care, research specifically examining how activity levels relate to hearing outcomes in this community is limited. This dashboard aims to help surface that gap, using real NHANES data to explore patterns that could inform more targeted research and outreach.
